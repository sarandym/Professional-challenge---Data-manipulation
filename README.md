I handled data preparation and ingestion via VS Code, an IDE (Integrated Development Environment) for coding. I used libraries like Pandas, DataFrames, and NumPy, which ensure performance with more compact lines of code. These are crucial tools for Big Data analysis using Python programming.

Sales per Hour Chart
The "sales per hour" chart groups the quantity of sales at a specific hour over 7 days a week. This allows us to identify peak sales times.
We see that the chart follows a "zigzag" pattern, likely due to how the AI created the file. It did not consider real-world data, where there would be a sales peak during workers' leisure hours and a dip during business hours. Regardless, we will analyze the data as is.
The peak of sales was at 8:00 AM. Following that, Noon and 6:00 PM lead as the times with the most sales, respectively.

Analysis Results
Curiously, the times customers use the app most are even hours (based on the sales chart).

The marketing team can work on promotions for "sneakers" and advertise during peak sales hours (8:00 AM, 12:00 PM) to convert cart additions into sales.

The demand for "caps" is high. Depending on stock levels, we can apply the concept of supply and demand and increase their price.

Marketing actions are necessary for "t-shirts"; they hold a good position in the cart ranking but are not selling.

The "watch" is one of the most converted into sales, but in the views chart, it ranks 9th. To sell more, it is suggested to place a watch advertisement on the homepage so this item is seen more.
